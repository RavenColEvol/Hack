About VCET HACKATHON

VCET Hackathon is a 30 hours Hackathon organized by Department of Information Technology of "Vidyavardhini's College of Engineering and Technology". By organizing this Hackathon, we are trying to promote a strong Programming and Product Building Culture among students that will help them develop Problem Solving, Critical Thinking and Software Development Skills. It is an experience to take on challenging problems we are surrounded by all the time and crack them down. Will you get time to eat? Can you ditch your sleep? Do you have passion to build? Find out in this 30 hours long thrilling experience!

Event Guideline

RULES

  • The VCET Hackathon will take place on 27th September, 2019
  • A team can consist of 1 to 4 members. Every member of the team must be a student of an university or a college. A team can have members from different colleges.
  • Entry fees is Rs.500 per member.
  • Participants are free to use open standard repositories, APIs and publically available libraries.
  • Participants will have to bring their laptops. Participants will not be allowed to take their machines out of the campus during Hackathon.
  • Participants are allowed to go home only after 30 hours of coding session followed by pitching session. We will provide resting area (separate for male and female participants).
  • Progress of the projects will be tracked during Hackathon. Use of readymade code or precompiled code will lead to disqualification.
  • No storage devices such as Pen Drives, Hard Drives etc. are allowed.
  • Participants are expected to behave professionally and responsibly.
  • Decisions made by organizers and judges are final.

THE INITIAL STAGE !

This was the first time that VCET was organising Hackathon and which went on to be one of the most successful events of the college. VCET's Hackathon 2017 saw 23 teams consisting of 65 participants competing against each other for 30 hours showcasing their Coding prowess to solve a Real World problem through their working prototypes.
The final day saw the 6 finalists pitching their unique ideas with working solutions before the judges. Out of which the Top 3 teams emerged as the winners of the very first edition of VCET's Hackathon winning cash prizes worth 40,000 INR.

SECOND ANNUAL !

Hackathon 2018 which was followed by the next year turned out much more successful than the previous year. This year the number of teams went up to 36 from 23, which was in the Inaugural year. Participants came up with solutions to real world problems by showcasing their Coding prowess.
Apart from monetary prizes, students got Internship as well as an enriching experience of building a working prototype which gives a great credibility to the designing and development skills of the participants and adds value to their resume.
